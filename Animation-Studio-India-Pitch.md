# Indie Animation Studio India — Investor Pitch Document
### Luminary Frame Studios (Proposed)
**Prepared:** March 2026 | **Confidential**

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Luminary Frame Studios is a proposed indie animation studio to be incorporated in Pune, Maharashtra, targeting original IP creation for Indian and international OTT platforms, alongside B2B animation services for global clients. The studio seeks a **Series Seed raise of ₹3.5 crore (~$420K)** to fund an 18-month runway through first IP delivery.

The Indian animation market was valued at **$1.89–2.4 billion in 2024** and is projected to reach **$14.7–25.2 billion by 2030–32** at a CAGR of **35–38%**. With government policy tailwinds (Maharashtra AVGC-XR Policy 2025), rising OTT commissioning, and India's structural cost advantage, the timing for an indie studio with a dual revenue model is compelling.

### 1.2 Demand Drivers

**OTT Platform Expansion:**
- Netflix and Amazon accounted for **53% of all global SVOD commissions** in Q1 2024 (Ampere Analysis)
- Amazon commissioned a **record 37 India-original titles** in Q1 2024 alone
- Netflix has identified India as its likely **largest APAC subscriber hub** and opened a creative technology hub in Hyderabad
- Indian OTT platforms (JioCinema, Disney+ Hotstar, SonyLIV, ZEE5) are each building dedicated animation content slates

**Post-Strike Recovery:**
- The 2023 Hollywood WGA/SAG-AFTRA strikes caused a ~9% revenue dip in India's animation sector as outsourced production froze. As of 2025, backlogs are flowing into Indian studios — creating a demand surge window.

**Domestic Content Demand:**
- India's 1.4 billion population, 650M+ internet users, and the growth of regional-language OTT consumption have created a large addressable market for mythology-based, folk tale, and culturally rooted Indian animated IP — a niche where Western studios cannot compete organically.

### 1.3 Why Indie, Why Now?

- Large Indian studios (Green Gold Animation, Tata Elxsi, DQ Entertainment) focus on service contracts at scale. There is a structural gap in **small agile studios** producing original IP at lower episode budgets ($50K–$150K/episode Indian production vs. $300K–$1M+ in the US).
- AI tools (Adobe Firefly, Runway Gen-3, Kling) are compressing pre-production and ideation timelines by 30–50%, partially offsetting India's already-low cost base.
- Maharashtra's AVGC-XR Policy 2025 grants **"industry status"** to animation studios, enabling capital subsidies, 24/7 operations, and lower electricity tariffs — reducing OpEx for a new entrant.

## 2. BUSINESS MODEL

### 2.1 Dual Revenue Architecture

**Pillar 1 — Original IP (60% of projected Year 3 revenue)**
- Develop 2D animated original series targeting Indian mythology, historical adventure, and folk-tale genres
- License to Indian OTT platforms (upfront + royalty model)
- Pursue co-production deals with international buyers seeking non-Western IP
- Merchandising rights retained for top-performing IP

**Pillar 2 — B2B Services (40% of projected Year 3 revenue)**
- Animation outsourcing for US/European/Australian studios
- Motion graphics and explainer videos for Indian EdTech and corporate clients
- Ad animation for FMCG and D2C brands
- Provides cash-flow stability while IP is in development

## 5. RED FLAG ANALYSIS

The following are investor-critical risk factors that demand diligence and mitigation planning:

### Red Flag 1 — IP Development Timeline Risk (HIGH)
**Risk:** Original series development takes 18–30 months from concept to first episode delivery. During this period, the studio generates zero IP revenue and burns seed capital.
**Evidence:** Benchmark: a 26-episode 2D animated series for an Indian OTT platform typically requires 12–18 months in pre-production alone (bible, scripts, designs) and another 6–12 months in production.
**Mitigation:** Service revenue (B2B pillar) is projected to cover ~60% of OpEx in Year 1. IP work begins only after 6 months of service pipeline is secured.

### Red Flag 2 — OTT Platform Gatekeeping (HIGH)
**Risk:** Indian OTT platforms (Disney+ Hotstar, Netflix India, SonyLIV) tend to commission animation from studios with established track records. A new studio may struggle to get in the room.
**Evidence:** OTT platforms in India typically require 2–3 produced samples, a full show bible, and a distribution track record before greenlighting a commission.
**Mitigation:** Target short-form (YouTube/Reels-first) distribution for pilot IP. Use festival circuit (Annecy Kids, Asian Animation Summit) for international co-production introductions. Budget for one unsolicited pilot production.

### Red Flag 3 — Talent Concentration Risk (MEDIUM-HIGH)
**Risk:** A 10-person indie studio is existentially dependent on 2–3 key creatives. Departure of the Creative Director or Lead Animator mid-production can blow budgets and timelines.
**Evidence:** Indian animation industry salary surveys (2024) note aggressive poaching of senior talent by global remote roles offering 2–4x Indian studio rates.
**Mitigation:** Equity-based retention (ESOPs), multi-year contracts with milestone vesting for key creatives, and documented IP ownership clauses.

### Red Flag 4 — B2B Service Pricing Erosion (MEDIUM)
**Risk:** AI animation tools (Runway, Kling, Adobe Firefly) are beginning to automate tasks that small studios charge per-hour/per-minute for. Explainer video and ad animation pricing may compress 30–50% by 2027.
**Evidence:** The global explainer video market has already seen 20–30% price compression from Southeast Asian and AI-assisted competitors (2023–2024 trends).
**Mitigation:** Move B2B positioning up the value chain toward creative strategy, character development, and IP-adjacent services rather than commodity production.

### Red Flag 5 — Government Policy Execution Risk (MEDIUM)
**Risk:** AVGC policies are new and have limited track records of actual disbursement. Capital subsidies and employment incentives may face bureaucratic delays of 12–24 months.
**Evidence:** India's startup subsidy disbursement history shows significant lag between policy announcement and fund release (e.g., Startup India incentive disbursements).
**Mitigation:** Build financial model with zero government subsidy assumption. Any subsidies received treated as upside, not base case.

### Red Flag 6 — IP Ownership Dilution (MEDIUM)
**Risk:** Co-production deals with foreign partners (Canada, UK, France, Australia) often require partial IP ownership transfer as a condition of funding access.
**Evidence:** Standard European co-production treaties require the foreign partner to hold 20–50% of underlying IP rights.
**Mitigation:** Structure co-production deals with geography-specific rights splits (retain India/South Asia rights); negotiate for format rights ownership.

### Red Flag 7 — Market Concentration (LOW-MEDIUM)
**Risk:** Revenue model in Year 2–3 is dependent on 2–3 OTT platform deals. Loss of one commission can swing EBITDA from positive to negative.
**Mitigation:** Pursue simultaneous development of at least 3 properties; never let any single client exceed 40% of revenue.

## 6. SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS

### 6.1 Key Value Drivers Identified

Three variables have the highest leverage on studio viability:

1. **OTT Episode Commission Rate** — price per episode received from OTT platforms
2. **B2B Utilisation Rate** — % of team capacity billed to external clients
3. **Salary Inflation Rate** — annual growth in team compensation costs

### 6.2 Year 3 EBITDA Sensitivity — OTT Commission Rate

Base case: ₹50L per episode, 6 episodes in Year 3

| Commission Rate per Episode | Year 3 Revenue (OTT) | Year 3 EBITDA | EBITDA Margin |
|-----------------------------|--------------------|---------------|---------------|
| ₹25L (bear — 50% cut) | ₹150L | ₹(10)L | Negative |
| ₹35L (downside) | ₹210L | ₹50L | 12.5% |
| **₹50L (base)** | **₹300L** | **₹160L** | **30.8%** |
| ₹70L (upside) | ₹420L | ₹280L | 40.6% |
| ₹100L (bull — INT'L deal) | ₹600L | ₹460L | 53.5% |

**Pressure Point:** If OTT commission rates fall below ₹35L/episode, the studio cannot achieve double-digit EBITDA margins on IP alone. B2B revenue must compensate.

### 6.3 Year 3 EBITDA Sensitivity — B2B Utilisation Rate

Base case: 70% utilisation on 8 billable team members at blended rate ₹1L/minute equivalent

| B2B Utilisation | Year 3 B2B Revenue | Year 3 EBITDA | EBITDA Margin |
|----------------|-------------------|---------------|---------------|
| 30% (critical low) | ₹95L | ₹35L | 8.7% |
| 50% (downside) | ₹145L | ₹85L | 18.9% |
| **70% (base)** | **₹220L** | **₹160L** | **30.8%** |
| 85% (upside) | ₹265L | ₹205L | 35.7% |

**Pressure Point:** Below 50% B2B utilisation, EBITDA margin falls to sub-20%. The studio becomes cash-flow negative if both OTT commission AND utilisation disappoint simultaneously (see combined stress below).

### 6.4 Year 3 EBITDA Sensitivity — Salary Inflation

Base case: 15% annual salary inflation (industry benchmark for Indian animation talent)

| Annual Salary Inflation | Year 3 Salary Cost | Year 3 EBITDA | EBITDA Margin |
|------------------------|-------------------|---------------|---------------|
| 5% (benign) | ₹120L | ₹200L | 38.5% |
| **15% (base)** | **₹145L** | **₹160L** | **30.8%** |
| 25% (stress) | ₹175L | ₹130L | 25.0% |
| 40% (severe — talent war) | ₹215L | ₹90L | 17.3% |

**Pressure Point:** Salary inflation above 25% per year (plausible in 2025–2027 as global studios hire Indian talent remotely) materially compresses margins and may trigger a talent crisis.

### 6.5 Combined Stress Scenario (Bear Case)

| Scenario | OTT Rate | B2B Utilisation | Salary Inflation | Year 3 EBITDA |
|----------|----------|----------------|-----------------|---------------|
| Bull | ₹70L | 85% | 5% | ₹370L |
| Base | ₹50L | 70% | 15% | ₹160L |
| Mild Stress | ₹35L | 60% | 20% | ₹55L |
| Bear | ₹25L | 45% | 30% | **(₹95L)** |
| Catastrophic | ₹0 (no OTT deal) | 35% | 35% | **(₹195L)** |

**Conclusion:** The studio survives a mild stress scenario but requires additional capital in a bear scenario. A catastrophic scenario (no OTT deal + low utilisation) would exhaust seed capital and require a bridge round by Q3 Year 2.

## 9. CONCLUSION

Luminary Frame Studios is positioned at the intersection of India's animation cost advantage, an underserved original IP market, and a rapidly improving policy environment. The dual-revenue model reduces binary risk. The principal risks — OTT commissioning rates, talent retention, and B2B pricing erosion — are manageable with the right operational discipline and are transparent in the sensitivity analysis above.

**The bull case is compelling. The bear case is survivable with a pre-planned bridge.**

The ₹3 crore seed raise buys 18 months of runway, two produced pilots, and a B2B services business that partially self-funds. That is the minimum viable proof of concept to raise a Series A.
