# Japan Indie Anime Studios — Base Reference List
*Research Date: March 2026 | Purpose: Animation Studio Pitch — Comparable Universe*

---

## Selection Criteria
- Small/independent Japanese animation studios
- Active as of 2019 (survived the 2018 mass-closure wave)
- Low revenue, quality-over-volume output model
- OVA production in core business DNA
- NOT bankrupt/dissolved

---

## Final Shortlist — Surviving Indie Studios

### 1. Studio Colorido
- **Founded:** August 2011, Setagaya, Tokyo
- **Founder:** Hideo Uda (ex-Gonzo, ex-Khara)
- **Parent:** Twin Engine (subsidiary since ~2016) + Netflix co-production deal (2022)
- **Model:** Boutique feature films + ONA shorts + B2B background art + commercials
- **Output pace:** ~1 feature/ONA every 18–24 months
- **Key works:** *Penguin Highway* (2018), *A Whisker Away* (2020), *Drifting Home* (2022), *My Oni Girl* (2024)
- **OVA/Short work:** *Pokémon: Twilight Wings*, *Pokétoon* (Nintendo/TPCi)
- **Revenue signal:** *Penguin Highway* — ¥307M domestic BO from a small-team production
- **Margin thesis:** Multiple revenue streams (B2B art + commercials + originals) cross-subsidise films; Netflix deal reduces distribution risk

### 2. Lapin Track
- **Founded:** June 30, 2014, Nishi-Ogikubo, Tokyo
- **Founders:** Teruko Utsumi (ex-Brain's Base screenwriter) + Masakazu Watanabe (ex-Brain's Base/MAPPA producer)
- **Parent:** Independent (LLC structure)
- **Model:** Co-production support → OVA-led originals post-2019
- **Output pace:** ~1 title/year; almost exclusively OVA or co-production support
- **Key works (lead):** *Onna no Sono no Hoshi* OVA (2022), *Undead Girl Murder Farce* (2023), *SHOSHIMIN* (2024), *You and I Are Polar Opposites* (2026)
- **OVA focus:** Most OVA-pure studio on the list; bundled manga-edition OVA model
- **Margin thesis:** Support work (key animation for *Sarazanmai*, *Akiba Maid War*) cross-subsidises originals; zero broadcast overhead

### 3. ZEXCS
- **Founded:** January 23, 1998, Koganei, Tokyo
- **Founder:** Tomoko Kawasaki (ex-J.C.Staff producer)
- **Parent:** Independent; sister company Feel (shared production overhead)
- **Model:** Selective TV series + OVA work; experimental/artistic direction
- **Output pace:** 1–2 productions/year; 12 OVAs over 20 years
- **Key works:** *Chrome Shelled Regios*, *The Flowers of Evil* (2013, full rotoscope), *Sukitte Ii na yo*, *Shadowverse F* (2024)
- **OVA track:** 12 OVAs (1999–2018) — pilot episodes + manga-bundled supplements
- **Margin thesis:** 26-year survival through two major industry downturns (2002 dot-com, 2018 closure wave); Feel partnership reduces fixed costs; selective output = disciplined capital allocation

### 4. Lay-duce
- **Founded:** August 9, 2013, Nakano, Tokyo
- **Founder:** Noritomo Yonai (ex-Bones A Studio producer)
- **Parent:** Twin Engine (partnership since ~2018) + Netflix relationship (2024)
- **Model:** Character-driven TV series + OVAs; artist-development focus
- **Output pace:** ~1 title/year; no volume chasing
- **Key works:** *Classroom Crisis* (2015), *Magi: Sinbad no Bouken* (2016), *O Maidens in Your Savage Season* (2019), *Tomo-chan Is a Girl!* (2023), *Rising Impact* (Netflix, 2024)
- **OVA work:** Supplemental OVAs bundled with franchise titles; Haikyu!! collaboration
- **Margin thesis:** Bones A Studio pedigree → quality at small scale; Twin Engine backing creates financial floor; Netflix emerging as distribution partner

## Industry Context (2018–2020 Closure Wave)
- 2018: Record **16 studio closures** (7 bankruptcies + 5 dissolutions + others)
- Primary causes: unpaid animators, labor shortages driving up wages, revenue below break-even
- 2020: Sharp drop in closures (partly COVID disruption of legal proceedings)
- 2025: 8 more closures (Jan–Sep per Teikoku Databank) — industry instability ongoing
